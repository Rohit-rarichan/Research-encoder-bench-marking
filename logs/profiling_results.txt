================================================================================
PARAMETER COUNT CLARIFICATION
================================================================================

The parameter counts reported above (e.g. ResNet-101: 127.9 M) are for the
full model: backbone encoder + UPerNet decoder head combined.

Backbone-only parameter counts (standard published values):

  SegFormer MiT-B0  :   3.7 M   (backbone only)
  ResNet-101        :  44.5 M   (backbone only)
  Swin-B            :  87.8 M   (backbone only)
  ConvNeXt-B        :  88.6 M   (backbone only)

Why the UPerNet head adds so many params for ResNet-101:
  ResNet-101's last stage outputs 2048 channels. The UPerNet PPM bottleneck
  is a single Conv2d(4096, 2048, 3x3) layer — that one conv alone is ~75 M
  params, pushing the total to 127.9 M. Swin-B and ConvNeXt-B top out at
  1024 channels so their UPerNet head is much lighter (~24 M added).

Full model breakdown (backbone + UPerNet):

  SegFormer MiT-B0 + UPerNet  :   9.4 M   (3.7 M backbone + ~5.7 M head)
  ResNet-101       + UPerNet  : 127.9 M   (44.5 M backbone + ~83.4 M head)
  Swin-B           + UPerNet  : 111.9 M   (87.8 M backbone + ~24.1 M head)
  ConvNeXt-B       + UPerNet  : 112.7 M   (88.6 M backbone + ~24.1 M head)


================================================================================
RESHAPE LATENCY EXPLANATION
================================================================================

ConvNeXt-B and ResNet-101 are pure CNNs — they always work in [B, C, H, W]
format, so "reshape" is just the final output F.interpolate upsample (~0.04 ms).

SegFormer and Swin-B are vision transformers that repeatedly convert between
spatial feature maps and 1D token sequences:

  SegFormer : 4x OverlapPatchEmbed (overlapping conv -> flatten -> transpose)
              one per encoder stage, contributing ~0.55 ms total.

  Swin-B    : 1x PatchEmbed (initial tokenisation) + 3x PatchMerging
              (concatenate neighbouring tokens -> linear project) between
              stages, contributing ~0.45 ms total.


================================================================================
ENCODER RECOMMENDATION
================================================================================

  SegFormer   8.23 ms  -- fastest by a wide margin; recommended if mIoU holds
  ResNet-101 11.76 ms  -- slightly faster than ConvNeXt but bloated param count
  ConvNeXt-B 12.30 ms  -- best large-model option; faster than Swin-B by ~6 ms
  Swin-B     18.76 ms  -- slowest; hard to justify over ConvNeXt-B

  Recommendation:
    - If accuracy is competitive: go with SegFormer (fastest, lightest).
    - If a large backbone is needed: ConvNeXt-B (12.3 ms, same accuracy
      class as Swin-B but 35% faster, no tokenisation overhead).
    - Avoid Swin-B: slowest with no accuracy advantage over ConvNeXt-B.
    - Avoid ResNet-101: most of its 127.9 M params are in the UPerNet head,
      not the backbone doing useful work.
